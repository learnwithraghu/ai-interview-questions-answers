# Question 6: Transformer Architecture in LLMs

**Question:** What is the role of the Transformer architecture in modern LLMs?

**Answer:**
The Transformer architecture (introduced by Google in the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need") is the foundational building block of virtually all modern Large Language Models (like GPT, Claude, Llama).

**Core Roles and Innovations:**
1. **Self-Attention Mechanism:** This is the heart of the Transformer. It allows the model to look at all words in a sequence simultaneously and determine which words are most relevant to each other, regardless of their position. This enables the model to understand context and long-range dependencies (e.g., figuring out what "it" refers to in a long paragraph).
2. **Parallelization:** Before Transformers, RNNs (Recurrent Neural Networks) processed text sequentially (word by word), which was slow. Transformers process entire sequences simultaneously. This extreme parallelization allows them to be trained on massive datasets using modern GPUs efficiently.
3. **Contextual Understanding:** Because of self-attention and positional encoding (which keeps track of word order), the Transformer creates highly nuanced representations of language, understanding that the word "bank" means something different in "river bank" versus "bank account."

In modern LLMs (which are typically "decoder-only" Transformers), the architecture's primary role is to take a sequence of tokens, understand the deep contextual relationships between them, and predict the statistically most likely next token, scaling this simple mechanism up to achieve emergent reasoning capabilities.
