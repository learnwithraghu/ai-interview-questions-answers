# Question 2: The Role of Temperature

**Question:** What is the role of temperature in LLM generation?

**Answer:**
Temperature is a hyperparameter used to control the randomness and creativity of the text generated by a Large Language Model.

Technically, temperature scales the logits (the raw unnormalized scores for the next potential tokens) before they are passed through a softmax function to create probabilities.

- **Low Temperature (e.g., 0.0 - 0.3):**
  - The model becomes more deterministic and focused. It heavily favors the most likely next tokens.
  - **Use Cases:** Tasks requiring factual accuracy, structured data extraction (like JSON), coding, or following strict logical instructions.

- **High Temperature (e.g., 0.7 - 1.0+):**
  - The probability distribution flattens, making the model more likely to choose less probable tokens. This leads to more diverse, creative, and sometimes unpredictable outputs.
  - **Use Cases:** Creative writing, brainstorming, poetry, or generating diverse marketing copy.

Setting the temperature to exactly 0 makes the model fully greedy, almost always picking the single highest-probability token.
